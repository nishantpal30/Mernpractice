Shaheed Bhagat Singh

“They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit.”
                                          — Shaheed Bhagat Singh

Introduction

Bhagat Singh is not just a name, but a symbol of courage, sacrifice, and revolution. Born in an era when India was shackled under British colonial rule, he chose the path of fearless resistance to liberate the nation. Even at a young age, he embraced martyrdom for a cause that transcended fear and selfishness: freedom for all.

Early Life

  • Born: September 28, 1907
  • Birthplace: Banga village, Lyallpur district, Punjab Province (now in Pakistan)
  • Parents: Kishan Singh Sandhu and Vidyavati Kaur
  • Family: A Sikh family with a history of patriots—his uncle Ajit Singh was also a freedom fighter.

From childhood, Bhagat Singh was influenced by stories of revolutionaries and national heroes. The massacre at Jallianwala Bagh (1919), which he visited the very next day, left a permanent mark on his conscience.

Turning into a Revolutionary

  • Joined Movements:
    • Non-Cooperation Movement (Gandhi's call, 1921)
    • Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA)
